How to Compress a PDF in Firefox

Firefox is the most privacy-focused mainstream browser — and it pairs well with ShrinkPDF, which processes your PDFs locally without uploading them to any server. This guide covers how to compress a PDF in Firefox on desktop (Windows, Mac, Linux) and on Android. No add-ons, no extensions, no sign-up required.

Compress PDF in Firefox on Desktop (Windows, Mac, Linux)

1

Open a New Tab

Press Ctrl+T (Windows/Linux) or Cmd+T (Mac) to open a new tab. Type shrinkpdf.fyi in the address bar and press Enter.

2

Upload Your PDF

Click Choose PDF File or drag and drop a PDF directly onto the upload area. Firefox supports drag and drop from Windows Explorer, macOS Finder, or any file manager on Linux. The file stays on your device — nothing is sent to a server.

3

Select Compression Level and Compress

Choose Balanced for email and everyday sharing, or Maximum when you have a strict file size limit (government portals, university submissions). Click Compress PDF Now and wait a few seconds.

4

Download the Compressed PDF

Click Download. Firefox shows a download notification in the top-right corner. The file saves to your default Downloads folder. You can change the download location in Firefox Settings → General → Downloads → Save files to.

Firefox tip: drag and drop from the Downloads panel

If you've just downloaded a PDF from another site and want to compress it immediately, open Firefox's Downloads panel (Ctrl+J / Cmd+J), and drag the file directly from the panel onto the ShrinkPDF upload area. No need to open File Explorer or Finder.

Compress PDF in Firefox on Android

1

Open Firefox and Go to ShrinkPDF

Open the Firefox app on your Android device. Tap the address bar and type shrinkpdf.fyi. The full compression tool loads in the mobile browser — no app installation needed.

2

Upload Your PDF

Tap Choose PDF File. Firefox for Android opens the system file picker. Navigate to your Downloads folder or browse to wherever the PDF is stored. Select the file to upload it.

3

Compress and Download

Select a compression level, tap Compress PDF Now, and wait for the result. Tap Download when ready. Firefox on Android saves the file to Internal Storage → Downloads. A notification appears — tap it to open the file directly or share it to another app.

Compressing a PDF Already Open in Firefox

Firefox has a built-in PDF viewer that can display, annotate, and print PDFs — but it cannot compress them. If you have a PDF open in Firefox's viewer and want to compress it:

  1. Click the download icon in Firefox's PDF toolbar (top right of the viewer) to save the file to your device
  2. Open a new tab and go to shrinkpdf.fyi
  3. Upload the saved PDF, choose a compression level, and download the compressed version

There is no way to compress a PDF directly from inside Firefox's built-in viewer — the save-then-compress workflow is always required.

Firefox + ShrinkPDF: A Privacy-First Combination

Firefox is built around privacy — it blocks third-party trackers by default and is the only major browser not owned by an advertising company. ShrinkPDF shares a similar philosophy: your PDF is processed entirely in your browser, with zero server upload at any point.

You can verify this yourself in Firefox:

  1. Press F12 to open Firefox DevTools
  2. Click the Network tab
  3. Upload and compress a PDF
  4. Observe that no outgoing requests contain your file data — only small analytics calls are visible
Why this matters for sensitive documents

Tools like Smallpdf and iLovePDF upload your file to their servers to perform compression. ShrinkPDF processes everything locally using JavaScript. For sensitive documents — tax files, medical records, identity documents, signed contracts — the distinction is meaningful. Firefox's Enhanced Tracking Protection plus ShrinkPDF's local processing means your document data goes nowhere it shouldn't.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does Firefox have a built-in PDF compressor? ▼
No. Firefox can view, annotate, and print PDFs through its built-in PDF viewer, but it cannot compress them. To compress in Firefox, open ShrinkPDF as a website in a new tab — no add-on or installation required.
Do I need a Firefox add-on to compress PDFs? ▼
No. ShrinkPDF is a website that runs directly in your Firefox tab. No add-ons, extensions, or plugins are needed. This also means it works on managed devices, school computers, or work systems where installing browser extensions may be restricted.
Where does Firefox save downloaded files on Windows? ▼
Firefox saves downloads to C:\Users\[username]\Downloads by default. You can change this in Firefox Settings → General → Downloads. You can also choose 'Always ask you where to save files' if you prefer to pick a location each time.
Does ShrinkPDF work in Firefox for Android? ▼
Yes — ShrinkPDF works in Firefox for Android the same way it does on desktop. Open shrinkpdf.fyi in Firefox on Android, upload your PDF, compress it, and download the result. The compressed file saves to Internal Storage → Downloads.
Is ShrinkPDF compatible with Firefox's strict privacy settings? ▼
Yes. ShrinkPDF works correctly with Firefox's Enhanced Tracking Protection enabled, including Strict mode. The compression itself has no dependency on third-party scripts or cookies — it runs entirely on local JavaScript. Only optional analytics scripts may be blocked by strict settings, which does not affect the tool's functionality.
Can I compress a PDF in Firefox Focus? ▼
Firefox Focus blocks most scripts by default, which may interfere with ShrinkPDF's JavaScript-based compression. For the best experience, use standard Firefox rather than Firefox Focus for PDF compression tasks.
