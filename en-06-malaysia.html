File Size Limits by Portal

Malaysian government portals enforce strict upload limits to manage server load. The limits below are based on current portal requirements as of 2026:

Upload Limits by Portal
  • LHDN e-Filing (ezHASiL): 2MB per supporting document
  • UPU / MyGovUC (university application): 2MB per file
  • JPA Scholarship Portal (Biasiswa JPA): 2MB per document
  • EPF i-Akaun (KWSP): 1–2MB per file depending on document type
  • SPA Online (Suruhanjaya Perkhidmatan Awam): 2MB per document
  • MARA / Bank Rakyat scholarship portals: 1–2MB per file
  • MySejahtera / Padu: 2MB per upload
  • MyIPO (intellectual property): 5MB per attachment

Most portals share the same 2MB threshold. EPF i-Akaun is stricter for certain document types — when in doubt, compress to under 1MB to be safe across all portals.

Why Are My Scanned Documents So Large?

A scanned document at the default scanner settings (300 DPI, colour) can easily produce a 5–15MB PDF for just a few pages. Here's why:

  • High DPI (dots per inch): 300 DPI is meant for printing, not web upload. At 150 DPI, text documents are still perfectly readable on screen.
  • Colour scanning: Colour adds 3x more data than black and white. For official documents like salary slips or transcripts, colour is rarely needed.
  • No compression applied: Default scanner apps on phones (including CamScanner, Adobe Scan) often save in high quality by default.
  • Multiple pages combined: A 6-month bank statement scanned at 300 DPI colour can exceed 10MB easily.

The good news: these factors mean your documents have a lot of room to compress without losing legibility.

Portal-Specific Tips

LHDN e-Filing (ezHASiL)

LHDN requires supporting documents during tax filing for claims like medical expenses, education fees, and donations. The most commonly rejected files are:

  • EA Forms scanned in colour at 300 DPI
  • Medical receipts with photos or logos that inflate file size
  • Combined bank statements across multiple months

For LHDN, aim for under 1.5MB per file to have a safe margin. Split multi-month statements into individual months if needed.

UPU / MyGovUC (University Application)

SPM results, co-curricular certificates, and personal statements are commonly too large. Certificates printed in colour and scanned at high DPI are the usual culprits. Since these documents have no fine print that requires high resolution, 150 DPI black-and-white compression works perfectly.

JPA Biasiswa Scholarship Portal

JPA requires income proof, transcripts, and supporting letters. Income documents like payslips and bank statements can get large when combined. Upload them separately rather than merging into one PDF, and compress each individually.

EPF i-Akaun (KWSP)

EPF is one of the stricter portals — some document types have a 1MB limit. Compress your documents to under 800KB if you're submitting to EPF to avoid rejection. IC copies and bank account documents are the most common uploads here.

SPA Online (Civil Service Application)

SPA requires academic certificates, transcripts, and experience letters. These are typically multi-page documents and can reach 5–8MB when scanned. Use maximum compression and consider splitting certificates from transcripts into separate uploads.

Tips Before You Even Scan

If you haven't scanned your documents yet, these settings will save you the compression step entirely:

  • Use 150 DPI instead of 300 DPI — sufficient for official documents, reduces file size by ~75%
  • Scan in black and white (grayscale) — most Malaysian government documents don't require colour; this alone cuts size by 60–70%
  • Use a scanner app with built-in compression — Microsoft Lens (free) and Google PhotoScan both produce smaller files than most scanner apps by default
  • Scan pages individually if possible — it's easier to manage file sizes per document

Still Too Large After Compression?

For documents that still exceed the portal limit after maximum compression, try these steps:

  • Split the document: Use the ShrinkPDF Split tool to break a multi-page PDF into separate files, then compress and upload each part individually.
  • Re-scan at lower DPI: If you have access to the original physical document, re-scan at 100–150 DPI in black and white. This almost always results in a file well under 1MB per page.
  • Remove unnecessary pages: Use the Reorder tool to delete blank pages or duplicates before compressing.
  • Convert to black and white first: If your document was scanned in colour, converting it to grayscale before compression significantly improves the compression ratio.
