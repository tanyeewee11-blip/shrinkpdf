Who Needs to Add Page Numbers?

Numbered pages make a document easier to reference, print, and keep in order — here's when it matters most.

📚
Reports and Theses

Academic submissions, dissertations, and formal reports are usually expected to have consistent page numbering, often starting after a cover page or table of contents.

📋
Contracts and Agreements

Numbered pages make it easy to reference a specific clause during discussion or signing, and confirm no pages are missing from a printed copy.

🖨️
Printed Handouts

When you print and physically hand out a multi-page document, page numbers keep pages in order if they get shuffled or dropped.

📤
Portal Submissions

Some government and university portals expect page numbers on multi-page PDF submissions such as appeals, applications, or supporting documents.

How to Add Page Numbers — Step by Step

1
Upload Your PDF

Click "Choose PDF File" or drag and drop. The tool reads the total page count so it can calculate the numbering.

2

Place numbers at the bottom centre, bottom right, or top centre. Pick a format — plain numbers, "Page 1", or "1 of 10" style.

3
Set the Starting Number

Start from 1, or from any other number if this document continues from a previous one. You can also skip numbering on a cover page.

4
Download the Numbered PDF

Everything is processed locally in your browser — no upload, no server. Download the finished file with numbers on every page.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I start numbering from a page other than 1?
Yes. Set the "Start numbering at" field to any number — useful when this document continues the numbering of a previous file, such as an appendix.
Can I skip numbering on a cover page?
Yes. Use the "Skip cover page" field to leave the first page (or first few pages) unnumbered while the rest of the document numbers normally.
What does the "1 of 10" format mean?
It shows both the current page number and the total page count, such as "3 / 10", which is useful so readers know how many pages remain.
Where can I place the page number?
Choose bottom centre (the most common placement for printed documents), bottom right, or top centre.
Is my file uploaded to a server?
No. Page numbers are drawn onto the PDF entirely inside your browser using JavaScript. The file is never uploaded or sent to any server.
