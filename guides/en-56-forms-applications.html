How to Compress a PDF for Forms, Applications & Submissions

HR onboarding paperwork, scholarship applications, and rental agreements all share the same problem: a scanned or signed PDF that's too large for the portal, email, or HR system you need to send it to. The compression steps are the same in each case, but what you should check afterward differs depending on whether the document has a signature, a stamp, or figures that need to stay sharp. This guide covers all three, plus what to know before compressing anything signed.

Before You Compress Anything Signed

What compression does and doesn't preserve

ShrinkPDF compresses a PDF by re-rendering each page as an image. Visually, this keeps clauses, dates, and figures readable at Balanced compression. But it does not preserve a cryptographic digital signature, and the output is no longer searchable or editable text. For an onboarding form, transcript, or agreement you've signed digitally through a proper e-signature platform, keep that originally-signed file as your record of truth, and only send the compressed copy where a visual copy satisfies the recipient's requirement (most portals and HR systems just need to read it, not cryptographically verify it). If you're not sure which applies to your situation, ask the recipient before relying on the compressed version alone.

With that in mind, here's the right level for each document type below.

HR Onboarding Documents

Starting a new job means submitting a stack of documents at once — offer letter, IC copy, certificates, and bank details. HR systems (Workday, SAP SuccessFactors, Oracle HCM) typically limit uploads to 2–10MB per file.

Typical onboarding checklist and the level to use for each
  • Signed offer letter / employment contract: Balanced — terms and signature should stay legible
  • IC / MyKad: Maximum — name and IC number just need to be readable
  • Academic transcripts and certificates: Balanced — grades and institution details are reviewed
  • Previous employment certificate: Balanced — job title, dates, and letterhead should be clear
  • EPF (KWSP) statement: Balanced — account number and figures are checked
  • Bank details / void cheque: Balanced — the account number must be exactly right
  • Medical examination report: Balanced — doctor's signature and findings should be clear
  • Emergency contact / personal data form: Maximum — text-only, no critical images
Double-check your bank account number

This is where your salary gets paid. After compressing with Balanced, re-open the file and confirm every digit of the account number is still clearly readable before submitting.

Scholarship & Academic Applications

Scholarship portals commonly limit uploads to 1–5MB per document. Transcripts and certificates scanned at high resolution can easily exceed that.

Which level for each document
  • Academic transcript: Balanced — grades, GPA, and subject names are the most important details in the whole application
  • SPM / A-Level / STPM certificate: Balanced — subject grades and the examining board's stamp need to stay legible
  • Co-curricular / achievement certificates: Balanced — organisation name and award details should be clear
  • Recommendation letter: Balanced — the referee's signature and letterhead matter
  • Personal statement / essay: Maximum is fine — plain text, no fine detail to preserve
  • IC / identity document: Maximum — name and IC number just need to be readable
  • Parents' payslip / income statement: Balanced — income figures are reviewed
Never submit a transcript you can't read clearly

A transcript with a blurry grade is worse than a slightly larger file. Check every grade after compressing. If anything looks unclear at Balanced, most portals will still accept the original file up to around 5MB — use that instead of pushing to Maximum.

Scholarship portals often slow down or time out near the deadline. Prepare and compress all documents a few days early rather than in the final hour.

Rental & Tenancy Agreements

A 10-page tenancy agreement scanned at full resolution can easily be 15–30MB, while agents and property portals typically want documents under 5MB.

Supporting documents and the level to use
  • The signed agreement itself: Balanced — keep signatures, initials, and clause text clearly legible
  • IC / identity document: Maximum — name and IC number just need to be readable
  • Payslips (3 months): Balanced — salary figures and employer name must be clear
  • Bank statements: Balanced — transaction history and balance figures are reviewed
  • Employment letter: Balanced — letterhead and stated salary matter
  • Utility bill (address proof): Maximum — name and address are the key details

Once compressed: attach directly to email (a Balanced 10-page agreement is usually 2–5MB, well within Gmail's 25MB limit), send as a Document (not as media) on WhatsApp, or upload to the portal's document field.

Frequently Asked Questions

Will compressing affect the legal validity of a signed agreement? ▼
Compression doesn't change any clause, date, or figure in the document. However, it does not preserve a cryptographic digital signature, since the page is re-rendered as an image rather than kept as a structured PDF. For an agreement signed through a proper e-signature platform, keep that originally-signed file as your record, and use the compressed copy only where a visual copy (one a person reads, rather than a system that verifies the signature) is acceptable.
Will compressing my transcript affect my grades? ▼
The grades, GPA, and subject names shown on the page don't change — but the compressed file is an image of the transcript, not searchable text. If the portal needs to read it visually, that's fine. If it needs to extract or search the text, check the original is also accepted. Always zoom in after compressing to confirm every grade is still clearly legible.
Should I compress before or after getting a document signed? ▼
After. Get the signature first — physical or digital — then compress the final signed file. Compressing first and adding a signature afterward means you're working from a different (already-flattened) file than the one you'll actually submit.
Is ShrinkPDF safe for confidential documents like payslips and signed contracts? ▼
Yes. ShrinkPDF processes files locally in your browser — salary figures, IC numbers, bank details, and contract terms are never sent to any server. This matters for exactly the kind of sensitive documents covered in this guide.
My document is still too large after Balanced compression. What now? ▼
Try Maximum — it typically reduces a Balanced file by another 30–50%. If it's still over the limit and the document is multi-page, use ShrinkPDF's Split tool to divide it into parts and check whether the portal accepts multiple uploads for that field.
