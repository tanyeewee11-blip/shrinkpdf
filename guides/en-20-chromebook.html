How to Compress a PDF on a Chromebook

The fastest method — works on every Chromebook without any setup:

1

Open Chrome and Go to ShrinkPDF

On your Chromebook, open the Chrome browser and go to shrinkpdf.fyi. The site loads fully in Chrome — no installation, no extension, no sign-in required.

2

Upload Your PDF

Click Choose PDF File. Your Chromebook's file picker will open. Navigate to your Downloads folder, Google Drive, or wherever the PDF is saved. Select the file.

3

Choose Compression Level

Select Balanced for most documents — reports, assignments, forms, and presentations. Select Maximum for scanned documents or when you need the absolute smallest file size.

4

Wait for Compression

ShrinkPDF processes the file in your browser. A typical 10MB PDF takes 5–15 seconds on a Chromebook. The compression runs entirely on your device — no uploading to any server.

5

Download the Compressed PDF

Click Download. The compressed PDF saves to your Chromebook's Downloads folder automatically. You'll see it in the bottom shelf tray or in the Files app under Downloads.

No Acrobat needed on Chromebook

Adobe Acrobat Pro doesn't have a native ChromeOS app, and the web version requires a paid subscription. ShrinkPDF gives you the same compression result for free, entirely in your browser, with no account needed.

Compressing a PDF Stored in Google Drive

If your PDF is saved in Google Drive rather than locally on your Chromebook:

1

Open the File Picker from ShrinkPDF

On the ShrinkPDF page, click Choose PDF File. In the file picker, look for Google Drive in the left sidebar. Click it to browse your Drive files.

2

Select the PDF from Drive

Find the PDF you want to compress and click Open. ChromeOS downloads it temporarily to process it in the browser.

3

Compress and Re-upload to Drive

After downloading the compressed file, open the Files app, find it in Downloads, right-click → Share → Save to Drive. Or drag it from Downloads into a Google Drive folder in the Files app.

What to Do After Compressing

Once your PDF is compressed and saved to Downloads, here are common next steps on a Chromebook:

  • Upload to Google Classroom: Open classroom.google.com, go to the assignment, click Add → File, and select the compressed PDF from Downloads.
  • Attach to Gmail: Compose an email, click the paperclip icon, and select the compressed PDF from Downloads.
  • Save to Google Drive: Open the Files app, find the file in Downloads, and drag it to the My Drive section.
  • Share via a link: Upload to Drive first, right-click the file → Get link → set to "Anyone with the link" → copy and paste into WhatsApp, Telegram, or email.

Why a Browser Tool Works Best on Chromebook

Chromebooks are designed to run web apps, not traditional desktop software. Most PDF compression tools are Windows or Mac apps that simply won't run on ChromeOS. Browser-based tools like ShrinkPDF are actually the native solution for Chromebook — they run in Chrome exactly as intended.

Does ShrinkPDF work offline on Chromebook?

ShrinkPDF requires an internet connection to load the page initially, but the compression itself runs entirely in your browser using local processing. Once the page is loaded, you can compress files even if your connection drops. However, for the most reliable experience, stay connected throughout.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I compress a PDF on a Chromebook without installing anything? ▼
Yes. ShrinkPDF runs entirely in the Chrome browser — no extensions, no Android apps, no Linux setup required. Open Chrome, go to shrinkpdf.fyi, and compress your PDF directly in the browser. The file is processed locally on your Chromebook and never sent to any server.
Where do downloaded files go on a Chromebook? ▼
Downloaded files are saved to the Downloads folder in the Files app. You can access it by opening the Files app (the folder icon in the shelf) and clicking Downloads in the left panel. The compressed PDF will appear there with the filename containing "compressed" or the original name depending on your browser settings.
Is there a PDF compressor app for Chromebook? ▼
There's no widely recommended standalone ChromeOS app for PDF compression. The most reliable approach is a browser-based tool like ShrinkPDF, which works natively in Chrome without requiring any app installation. Some Android PDF apps can be installed on Chromebook via the Play Store, but they often have limited functionality and require permissions you may not want to grant.
Can I use Linux on my Chromebook to compress PDFs? ▼
Yes, if your Chromebook supports Linux (Crostini), you can install command-line tools like Ghostscript to compress PDFs. However, this requires enabling Linux, installing packages via terminal, and running commands — much more complex than using ShrinkPDF in the browser. The browser approach is faster and easier for most users.
Why is my PDF so large on a Chromebook? ▼
PDFs created from Google Docs or Slides are usually small. Large PDFs on a Chromebook typically come from scanned documents saved to Drive, downloaded email attachments, or PDFs exported from external apps with high-resolution images. Compressing with ShrinkPDF typically reduces these by 60–85%.
