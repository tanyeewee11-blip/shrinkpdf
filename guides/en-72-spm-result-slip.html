✅ Check the MOE / SPM online portal first

The Ministry of Education Malaysia provides official digital copies of SPM and STPM results through the Semakan Keputusan portal. If your result slip is available there as a PDF download, it's already a small, clean, text-based PDF — often 200–500KB, which clears any portal limit without any compression needed. Log in with your IC number and download before scanning a physical copy.

Right-click the PDF → Properties (Windows) or Get Info (Mac). If it's already under 1.5MB, you may not need to compress — try uploading directly first.

2

3

Choose Balanced compression

Balanced is the right choice for result slips. It reduces a 3–5MB scan to typically 600KB–1.2MB — well under the 2MB limit — while keeping all subject codes, grades, and credit counts clearly readable. Only use Maximum if Balanced still leaves you over the portal limit.

4

Download and verify

Download the compressed file and open it. Zoom to 100% and verify your grades before submitting. A rejected application because of an illegible result slip is worth avoiding.

Critical Checks Before Submitting

Before uploading to any scholarship or university portal, confirm all of the following in your compressed result slip:

  • ✅ Your full name matches exactly as registered
  • ✅ IC number is clearly readable
  • ✅ All subject names are legible
  • ✅ Every grade (A+, A, B+, B, etc.) is clear and unambiguous
  • ✅ Credit count / number of passes is readable
  • ✅ The exam year and centre code are visible
  • ✅ Any official stamp or embossment (if applicable) is distinguishable
⚠️ Apply well before the deadline

UPU and scholarship portals get extremely slow during peak application periods. Prepare all your compressed documents — result slip, IC, certs, school recommendation letter — at least a week before the deadline. Don't be compressing files at 11pm the night before submissions close.

FAQ

Will UPU reject my application if the result slip was compressed? ▼
No. UPU and scholarship portals receive compressed documents regularly — they don't check how the PDF was prepared, only whether the content is legible and the file is within the size limit. As long as your grades, name, and IC number are clearly readable, a compressed PDF is completely acceptable.
I lost my physical result slip. Can I use a downloaded digital copy? ▼
Yes — the official digital PDF from the MOE portal is the authoritative document. It's actually better than a physical scan because it's a true text-based PDF (smaller, cleaner, always legible). Most portals accept it without any compression needed.
The portal also asks for co-curricular certs. Do I compress those the same way? ▼
Yes — use Maximum compression for co-curricular and achievement certificates. These are usually read for the certificate name and your name only, so aggressive compression doesn't affect their usability. This will typically bring most cert scans to under 300KB.
