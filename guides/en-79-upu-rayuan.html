Prepare Documents for UPU Rayuan (Appeal)

If UPU results didn't go the way you hoped, rayuan is the second chance — but the window to submit is short, usually around 10 days. This isn't the time to be scrambling with oversized scans or files in the wrong order. Here's what's typically needed and how to get it ready fast.

Know Your Window

Rayuan is time-limited — don't wait

The rayuan period typically opens on the same day results are announced and stays open for about 10 days. It's only available to candidates who received no offer at all, or who were only offered a certificate-level place at Politeknik, Kolej Komuniti, or ILKA rather than the diploma or degree level they applied for. Candidates who already accepted an offer generally cannot appeal to change programs.

Exact dates change every year and are announced on the official UPU Online portal (upu.mohe.gov.my) — check there directly rather than relying on last year's dates, since even a day's delay can mean missing the window entirely.

What Documents Are Usually Needed

Requirements vary by appeal reason, but commonly requested supporting documents include:

  • Identity card (MyKad) — front and back
  • SPM or STPM result slip
  • Co-curricular activity certificates
  • Family income information / supporting statement
  • Any additional supporting documents relevant to your specific appeal reason
Check the current year's exact list

Document requirements are set by UPU each session and can change. Confirm the current list on the official rayuan guidelines before you start scanning, so you don't waste time preparing something that isn't actually required — or miss something that is.

Compress Each Document First, Then Merge

If the rayuan portal's upload field expects one file covering multiple documents, compress each document individually before merging — not the other way around. Your IC scan and result slip need to stay legible, so they need a lighter compression level; a co-curricular certificate or supporting letter can usually take a more aggressive setting without anyone needing to read fine print off it. Merging everything first forces one compression level onto every page, which either blurs the documents that needed to stay sharp or leaves easy-to-compress pages larger than they needed to be.

The order that gives the smallest, clearest result

Compress each document at the level appropriate to it, merge the compressed files into one PDF in the correct order, then do a final compression pass on the merged file only if it's still slightly over the portal's limit.

Use ShrinkPDF's compress tool on each document first, then ShrinkPDF's merge tool to combine them in order — both run locally in your browser, so IC numbers and family income statements are never uploaded anywhere. See the full combine-and-compress workflow guide for the detailed step-by-step.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long do I have to submit a UPU rayuan (appeal)? ▼
The rayuan window is short — typically around 10 days from the day results are announced, and only open to candidates who received no offer, or only a certificate-level offer, at Politeknik, Kolej Komuniti, or ILKA rather than the university/diploma level they wanted. Always confirm the exact dates on the official UPU Online portal each year, since they change annually.
What documents are usually needed for a UPU rayuan? ▼
Commonly requested supporting documents include your identity card, exam result slip, co-curricular certificates, family income information, and any other supporting documents relevant to your appeal reason. Requirements can vary, so check the current rayuan guidelines on the official portal.
Should I combine my rayuan documents into one PDF or upload them separately? ▼
Follow whatever the portal's upload field asks for. Some fields accept only one file per document type; others expect a single combined PDF. If the portal wants one file covering several documents, compress each document at the appropriate level first, then merge them in order — merging first forces one compression level onto every document.
