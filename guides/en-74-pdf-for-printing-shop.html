✅ Most Malaysian printing shops use WhatsApp

The vast majority of kedai photostat in Malaysia accept orders via WhatsApp. WhatsApp allows files up to 100MB when sent as a "Document" (not via Gallery/Photos). Most documents — assignments, forms, reports — are well under 100MB and need no compression for sending via WhatsApp. Only compress if the file is genuinely over 100MB or if the shop's portal has a tighter limit.

The answer depends entirely on what you're printing:

⚠️ Never compress print-quality files

ShrinkPDF compresses by reducing DPI to screen resolution (72–96 DPI). Professional printing requires 300 DPI minimum. If you compress a brochure or photo for a print shop, it will print blurry — especially noticeable on photos and fine design elements. Keep the original full-resolution file for printing and only use a compressed version for digital sharing or portal submission.

Recommendations by Document Type

University assignments and reports

If your lecturer requires a printed copy and the file is large (e.g., a report with embedded charts and images), Light compression is safe. Printed text and charts look clear even at 120 DPI. Keep the original for digital submission.

Document photocopies and official letters

If you're sending documents to be photocopied (IC, certs, letters), the photocopy process itself introduces quality reduction. Light compression on the source PDF won't make a noticeable difference to the final photocopy. Send via WhatsApp as a Document.

CV and resume printing

Light compression is fine for printing a resume at a print shop. Even Balanced compression produces a result that prints clearly — text is sharp, layouts are preserved. Avoid Maximum for anything being printed.

Brochures and marketing materials

Do not compress. Send the full Canva or InDesign PDF at PDF Print quality (300 DPI). If it's too large to send via WhatsApp (over 100MB), share via Google Drive or USB drive instead of compressing and degrading print quality.

Sending via WhatsApp to a Printing Shop

1

Use "Document" not "Photos & Videos"

When attaching a PDF in WhatsApp, tap the 📎 icon → Document. Do not use the camera or gallery icon — that converts your PDF to images and will produce a much lower quality print.

2

Send the original if under 100MB

WhatsApp accepts files up to 100MB as a Document. If your PDF is under this limit, send the original without compression — especially for design files or photo prints.

3

If over 100MB, use Google Drive

Share a Google Drive link in the WhatsApp message instead. Most printing shops are familiar with this workflow. Never compress a print-quality file just to fit under WhatsApp's limit.

FAQ

The print shop said my file is too large. What should I do? ▼
First, ask if they accept Google Drive or USB — this avoids any size limit. If they need it via WhatsApp or their portal and the limit is under 100MB, check whether it's a text document (safe to use Light compression) or an image-heavy design file (share via Drive or USB instead of compressing). Never use Maximum compression for anything being physically printed.
Will Light compression affect how my document prints? ▼
For text documents, forms, and simple layouts, no — Light compression reduces DPI from 144–300 to about 120 DPI, which still prints cleanly for standard A4 documents. For photos, detailed graphics, or professional print jobs, yes — even Light compression can reduce print sharpness. When in doubt, don't compress for printing.
I sent a compressed PDF and the printout looks blurry. What happened? ▼
The compression reduced the DPI below what's needed for sharp printing. For a cleaner result, print from the original uncompressed file. If you don't have the original, you can re-export from the source application (Canva, Word, etc.) at full quality and send that version to the print shop.
