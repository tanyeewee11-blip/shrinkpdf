Best Free PDF Compressors

🗜️ ShrinkPDF (shrinkpdf.fyi)
100% FreeNo upload — local processingNo file size limitNo watermark

Compresses PDFs entirely in your browser — your file never leaves your device. Two compression levels: Balanced (96 DPI) and Maximum (72 DPI). No account, no ads in the tool itself, works on any device including mobile and Chromebook. Also includes merge, split, rotate, reorder, PDF-to-images, and images-to-PDF.

Best for: Anyone who values privacy, handles sensitive documents, or wants a no-friction tool. Ideal for government forms, IC/passport scans, payslips, and anything you wouldn't want on a third-party server.

📄 Smallpdf (smallpdf.com)
Free tier: 2 tasks/dayFiles uploaded to serverPaid plan available

One of the most well-known online PDF tools. The free tier allows 2 tasks per day with a file size limit. Files are uploaded to Smallpdf's servers. The interface is polished and it handles many PDF tasks beyond compression.

Best for: Occasional users who don't mind the daily limit and are okay with files being uploaded to a server.

📄 ilovepdf (ilovepdf.com)
Free with daily limitsFiles uploaded to serverPaid plan available

Similar to Smallpdf — a broad range of PDF tools with a free tier. Files are uploaded to their servers. The free tier has usage limits and occasionally shows ads. Useful for one-off tasks where privacy is not a concern.

Best for: Non-sensitive documents where you need a feature ShrinkPDF doesn't have (e.g. PDF to Word conversion).

Merge and Split PDFs

🔗 ShrinkPDF Merge & Split
FreeLocal processingNo limit

ShrinkPDF's Merge and Split tools handle both tasks in the browser with no upload. Merge combines multiple PDFs into one; Split extracts specific pages or splits by page range. Fast and private.

📑 PDF24 (pdf24.org)
FreeServer-side processingNo file size limit

A comprehensive free PDF tool with a desktop app option in addition to the web version. The desktop app processes files locally. The web version uploads to their servers. Good for batch operations across many files.

PDF Editing (Text)

True PDF text editing — changing existing text inside a PDF — is the hardest task to do for free. Most free tools have significant limitations here.

📝 LibreOffice Draw
Free & open sourceLocal — no uploadComplex layout may shift

LibreOffice is a free office suite that can open and edit PDFs in its Draw application. Basic text editing works; complex multi-column layouts or PDFs with many embedded images may not render correctly. Good for editing simple PDFs like forms or letters.

Download: libreoffice.org

📝 Google Docs (for simple PDFs)
FreeUpload to Google serversComplex layouts may break

Upload a PDF to Google Drive → right-click → Open with Google Docs. Google Docs converts the PDF to an editable document. Works well for text-heavy PDFs; may lose formatting on complex layouts. Re-export as PDF when done.

📊 PDF to Word / Excel
Limited free options

Converting PDF to editable Word or Excel is not well-supported by truly free tools. Options: Adobe Acrobat online (free tier, file uploaded), Smallpdf or ilovepdf (free tier with limits, uploaded to server), or LibreOffice (free, local, but may lose formatting). For occasional use, the free tier of Adobe's online tools works.

Signing PDFs

✍️ Adobe Acrobat Reader (Fill & Sign)
FreeFile may be uploaded

Adobe Reader (free) includes a Fill & Sign feature for adding text, checkboxes, and a drawn signature. Good for simple form filling and basic signatures. Not legally binding e-signatures — for that, you need Acrobat Sign or DocuSign.

✍️ Docusign (free tier)
3 signatures/month freeFiles uploaded to Docusign

The industry standard for legally binding e-signatures. The free tier allows 3 signature requests per month — enough for occasional personal use. Files are uploaded to DocuSign's servers.

A Note on Privacy

Most PDF tools are server-side — your file is uploaded, processed, and deleted. For documents like resumes or reports, this is usually fine. For sensitive documents — ICs, passports, bank statements, payslips, legal contracts — it's worth choosing a tool that processes locally.

How to tell if a tool uploads your file

Look for a progress bar labelled "Uploading..." or "Processing on server." If it appears, your file is being sent to their server. Tools that process locally (like ShrinkPDF) show progress without an upload step — the processing happens instantly in your browser.
