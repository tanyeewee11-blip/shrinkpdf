Choosing Position, Colour and Opacity

Opacity matters more than colour

The single biggest factor in whether a watermark looks professional or looks like it's ruining the document is opacity. Around 20-30% keeps text and images fully readable underneath while still being clearly visible. Higher opacity (50%+) is useful when the watermark itself is the point — for example, marking a sample document as unusable for anything but review.

Diagonal placement across the centre of the page is the standard choice for "CONFIDENTIAL" or "DRAFT" style watermarks — it's harder to crop out of a screenshot or printed copy than a small corner stamp. A bottom watermark works better for a subtler mark, such as a company name on a quotation, where you don't want it competing visually with the main content.

Gray is the safest default colour for most documents since it reads as neutral rather than urgent. Red draws more attention and suits genuinely time-sensitive labels like "VOID" or "EXPIRED".

Common Reasons to Watermark a PDF

Draft contracts and reports

Mark a document "DRAFT" while it's still under review, so nobody mistakes an early version for the final, signed copy. Once approved, watermark a clean final version or send the unwatermarked original.

Confidential internal documents

Stamp "CONFIDENTIAL" on financial statements, HR files, or internal reports before emailing them, so it's visually clear the file isn't meant to be forwarded freely.

Freelance and agency samples

Watermark portfolio samples, mockups, or quotations with a business name or "SAMPLE" so draft work isn't reused without payment.

Watermark, Then Compress or Number

Watermarking adds a small amount of page content, so file size barely changes — it's not a substitute for compression. If the watermarked file still needs to fit a portal's upload limit, run it through ShrinkPDF's Compress tool after watermarking (see the complete compression guide).

If the document also needs page numbers — common for contracts and reports — use the Add Page Numbers tool either before or after watermarking; the two don't interfere with each other since they're drawn in different positions on the page.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I remove a watermark once it's added? ▼
No. Once applied, the watermark is drawn as permanent page content and cannot be stripped back out through the tool. Keep your original unwatermarked file if you might need it later.
Will the watermark make my document hard to read? ▼
Not if you lower the opacity. A setting around 20-30% keeps the watermark visible without blocking the text or images underneath. Higher opacity is useful when you specifically want the watermark to stand out, such as marking a sample as unusable.
Does the watermark apply to every page automatically? ▼
Yes. The same text, position, colour and opacity are applied consistently to every page of the PDF in a single pass — there's no need to repeat the process per page.
Can I use my own text instead of "CONFIDENTIAL"? ▼
Yes. The text field accepts up to 60 characters of any wording — your company name, "DRAFT", "SAMPLE", "COPY", or a custom message.
Is it safe to watermark confidential documents? ▼
Yes. Watermarking happens entirely inside your browser using JavaScript — the file is never uploaded to a server, which matters for exactly the kind of sensitive documents (contracts, financial reports, ID scans) that people watermark.
