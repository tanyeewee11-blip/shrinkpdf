How to Compress a PDF to Send on Telegram

Telegram technically supports files up to 2GB — far more generous than WhatsApp. But large PDFs still cause problems: they're slow to send on mobile data, take ages to download for recipients, and eat into phone storage. Compressing before sending keeps things fast for everyone, especially in group chats where dozens of people download the same file.

Telegram's 2GB limit means most PDFs will send without hitting a size error. But "it sends" doesn't mean "it's practical" — a 50MB PDF takes minutes to download for someone on a 4G connection, and recipients in a large group may give up waiting.

How to Compress and Send a PDF on Telegram

1

Open ShrinkPDF in Your Browser

On your phone or computer, go to shrinkpdf.fyi. Works in Chrome, Safari, Firefox — any browser.

2

Upload the PDF

Tap Choose PDF File and select the document you want to send. On Android, this opens your Downloads or Files app. On iPhone, it opens the Files app.

3

Choose Compression Level

Select Balanced for documents with images. Select Maximum for scanned documents or text-heavy files where you want the smallest possible size.

4

Download the Compressed File

Tap Download. The compressed PDF saves to your Downloads folder.

5

Send on Telegram as a File

Open Telegram, go to the chat or channel, tap the paperclip icon (📎), select File, and choose the compressed PDF from your Downloads folder. Tap Send.

Telegram stores your files in the cloud

Unlike WhatsApp, files sent on Telegram are stored in Telegram's cloud. Recipients can re-download them anytime, even if they deleted the local copy. This means compressing before sending also saves your recipients' cloud storage quota (Telegram gives 1GB free for Telegram Premium users; standard accounts have unlimited cloud but files expire after a period in some older chats).

Send as File, Not as Media

Telegram gives you two ways to attach a PDF:

  • File: Sends the PDF as-is. Recipients download the original PDF and open it in a PDF viewer. This is what you want.
  • Photo/Video (Media): Telegram converts the file. PDFs sent as "media" may be compressed or converted differently. Always use File for PDFs.

To send as a File on mobile: tap the paperclip → File (not "Gallery" or "Photo"). On desktop: drag and drop → choose "Send as file" if prompted.

Recommended PDF Size for Telegram

Practical size targets by use case
  • 1-on-1 chats, good connection: Under 10MB is fine. Compress if larger.
  • Group chats (10–100 people): Under 5MB. Every member downloads it — smaller = faster for everyone.
  • Channels (100+ subscribers): Under 2MB. High download volume makes size matter more.
  • Scanned documents / invoices: Under 1MB. These are text-primary — Maximum compression is ideal.
  • Study materials / lecture notes: Under 3MB. Balanced compression keeps diagrams legible.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does Telegram compress PDFs automatically when I send them? ▼
No. Telegram sends files exactly as you upload them when you use the "File" option. The recipient downloads the same file at the same size. Unlike sending images (which Telegram compresses), files are sent at original quality and size.
What is Telegram's file size limit for PDFs? ▼
Telegram allows files up to 2GB per file. In practice, you're very unlikely to hit this with a PDF. However, practical download experience degrades for files over 10–20MB on mobile data, so compressing large PDFs before sending is still worthwhile.
Can I send a PDF in a Telegram channel after compressing? ▼
Yes. Compress the PDF with ShrinkPDF first, then post it to your Telegram channel by tapping the attachment icon → File → select the compressed PDF. Channel admins can also use Telegram Desktop to drag and drop files directly.
Why is my PDF slow to send on Telegram even with a fast phone? ▼
Upload speed on mobile networks is typically much slower than download speed — often 5–20 Mbps on 4G LTE. A 30MB PDF at 10 Mbps upload takes about 24 seconds just to upload. Compressing to 3MB cuts that to under 3 seconds. The bottleneck is your upload speed, not Telegram.
