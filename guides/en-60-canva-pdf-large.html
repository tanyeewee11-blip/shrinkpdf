Why Canva PDFs Are So Large

Canva is a design tool — it's built to produce visually rich output. When you export a PDF from Canva, every page is rendered as a full-resolution raster image at 144 DPI or higher, with all design elements (backgrounds, gradients, photos, illustrations) embedded as image data.

This is fundamentally different from a Word or Google Docs PDF, where text is stored as characters (tiny) and images are only embedded where you explicitly place them. A Canva page is entirely image data, even if it looks like it's "just text on a coloured background."

Why the "Print" export is always the largest

Canva's PDF Print export targets 300 DPI — print quality. A single A4 page at 300 DPI in full colour is roughly 8–26MB of raw image data before any compression. Canva applies JPEG compression internally, but even compressed, a single print-quality Canva page is typically 1–4MB. A 5-page resume or presentation can easily reach 15–20MB.

Fix 1: Compress After Exporting (Recommended)

The fastest and most reliable fix is to export from Canva normally, then run the PDF through ShrinkPDF. This takes about 30 seconds and typically reduces a Canva PDF by 70–85%.

1

Export from Canva

In Canva, click Share → Download → PDF Standard (not PDF Print, unless you specifically need print quality). PDF Standard is lower DPI and already produces smaller files.

2

Open ShrinkPDF

Go to shrinkpdf.fyi. The compression runs entirely in your browser — your design file never leaves your device.

3

Choose Maximum compression

For portal uploads (job applications, university submissions, government portals), Maximum compression is the right choice. For a 1-page resume, this typically brings a 10MB Canva PDF down to under 1MB — with no visible difference on screen.

4

Download and upload

Check the compressed file size in the result card. If it's within your portal's limit, download and upload. If it's still too large, see Fix 2 below.

⚠️ One thing to know about compressed Canva PDFs

After compression, the PDF is re-rendered as an image — text is no longer selectable or searchable. For a resume being submitted to a recruiter's email or portal, this doesn't matter. For a document that needs to be copy-pasted or searched by the recipient, share the original Canva PDF separately.

Fix 2: Change Export Settings in Canva

Before exporting, Canva gives you some control over file size. These settings don't eliminate the need for compression, but they reduce the starting size.

  • Choose PDF Standard, not PDF Print. Standard targets ~96–144 DPI (screen quality); Print targets 300 DPI (print quality). For anything being submitted digitally, Standard is appropriate and is 3–5× smaller than Print.
  • Flatten the PDF. In Canva's download dialog, enable "Flatten PDF" if the option appears. This merges all layers and removes embedded edit data, which can shave 10–30% off the file size.
  • Remove unnecessary pages. If your Canva design has multiple pages but you only need some of them, delete the others before exporting or use the Split PDF tool to extract the pages you need after exporting.
  • Remove background photos. High-resolution background photos are the single biggest contributor to Canva PDF size. If the design allows, replace photographic backgrounds with solid colours or simple gradients before exporting.

Fix 3: Export as JPG Instead (For Single Pages)

Some portals accept JPG images instead of PDFs. If that's an option, exporting directly from Canva as JPG at medium quality is often smaller than a compressed PDF.

In Canva: Share → Download → JPG → Quality: Medium (or 72 DPI). A single A4 page at medium quality JPG is typically 200–500KB — well under most portal limits. If you need multiple pages as a single file, use the Images to PDF tool to combine the JPG pages back into one PDF.

FAQ

Will compressing a Canva PDF make it look blurry? ▼
On screen at normal viewing size (100% zoom), a Maximum-compressed Canva PDF is visually indistinguishable from the original for most designs. The compression reduces DPI from 144+ down to around 72–96 DPI, which is still full screen resolution. You'd only notice a difference if you zoomed in to 400%+ on fine text or thin lines.
My Canva resume is 20MB. What compression level should I use? ▼
Maximum compression. A 20MB Canva resume will typically compress to 1.5–3MB on Maximum, which is under most job portal limits (usually 2–5MB). If it's still over the limit, try exporting from Canva as PDF Standard instead of PDF Print, then compress again.
Can I compress a Canva PDF with a logo or brand colours without the colours changing? ▼
Yes. The compression re-renders each page as a JPEG image, which means colours are preserved accurately. Slight colour shift can occur on very saturated or precise brand colours at Maximum compression — if colour accuracy matters, use Balanced compression instead, which preserves colours more faithfully at the cost of a slightly larger file.
Why is my 1-page Canva design 15MB but a 50-page Word document is only 500KB? ▼
Because they store content completely differently. Word exports text as character codes (a letter "A" is just a few bytes), while Canva exports every pixel of your design as image data. A Canva page is effectively a photograph — and photographs are large. This is true even if your design is "just text" on a solid background, because Canva renders it all as pixels.
