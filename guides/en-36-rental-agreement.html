How to Compress a PDF for Rental Agreement Submission

Signing a rental agreement generates a PDF that can be surprisingly large — a 10-page tenancy agreement scanned at full resolution can easily be 15–30MB. Agents, landlords, and property portals typically require documents under 5MB for email or upload. This guide covers how to compress a tenancy agreement without losing legibility of signatures, clauses, and dates.

The Key Rule: Always Use Balanced for Legal Documents

Never use Maximum compression on a signed agreement

Tenancy agreements are legal documents. Signatures, initials, dates, and clause text must be clearly readable — both now and if the document is ever needed for dispute resolution. Use Balanced compression only. Maximum compression can make handwritten signatures and small printed clauses harder to read clearly at normal zoom.

Step-by-Step: Compress a Tenancy Agreement PDF

1

Get Your Agreement as a PDF

If you signed physically: scan the signed agreement using your phone's scanner app (iOS Notes, Android Google Drive scan). Scan at 200 DPI — sufficient for text and signatures without producing an oversized file. If you signed digitally via an e-signature app: export or download it as a PDF directly from the app.

2

Open ShrinkPDF

Go to shrinkpdf.fyi. Your legal document is processed locally and never sent to any server.

3

Compress with Balanced

Upload the agreement PDF and select Balanced compression. Do not use Maximum for signed legal documents.

4

Verify Quality

Open the compressed PDF and check: (a) all signatures and initials are clearly visible, (b) all clause text is readable at 100% zoom, (c) all dates and rental amounts are legible, (d) any stamps or official seals are clear.

5

Send or Upload

Email to your landlord, agent, or relevant parties. Or upload to the property portal's document submission field.

Typical compression results for tenancy agreements

A 10-page scanned tenancy agreement at 300 DPI is typically 15–25MB. After Balanced compression: 2–5MB. After Maximum: 1–2MB (not recommended for signed documents). Balanced compression is sufficient to bring most agreements well under any portal or email limit.

Supporting Documents for Rental Applications

Besides the agreement itself, rental applications typically require several supporting documents. Here's what level to use for each:

  • IC / identity document: Maximum — name and IC number just need to be readable
  • Payslips (3 months): Balanced — salary figures and employer name must be clear
  • Bank statements: Balanced — transaction history and balance figures are reviewed
  • Employment letter: Balanced — company letterhead and stated salary matter
  • Utility bills (address proof): Maximum — name and address are the key details
  • Previous tenancy agreement: Balanced — for rental history verification

Sharing the Agreement After Compressing

Once compressed, you have several options for sharing with the landlord or agent:

  • Email: Attach directly — a Balanced-compressed 10-page agreement is typically 2–4MB, well within Gmail's 25MB limit
  • WhatsApp: Open WhatsApp → paperclip → Document → select the compressed PDF. Always send as Document, not as media
  • Property portal: Upload to the portal's document submission section
  • Google Drive link: If the landlord prefers a shareable link, upload to Drive and share — useful for large multi-party agreements

Frequently Asked Questions

Will compressing a tenancy agreement affect its legal validity? ▼
No. Compression reduces image resolution — it does not alter the content, clauses, or signatures in the document. A compressed PDF contains exactly the same legal text as the original. Using Balanced compression, signatures remain clearly visible and the document is legally equivalent to the original for all practical purposes.
My tenancy agreement is 20MB after scanning. How small can I get it? ▼
A 20MB scanned tenancy agreement typically compresses to 3–6MB with Balanced compression — a 70–85% reduction. This is well within email limits and most portal upload limits. If the portal still rejects it, ensure you're using Balanced (not keeping the original uncompressed) and that each page was scanned at 200–300 DPI rather than higher.
Should I compress before or after getting signatures? ▼
After. Always get all signatures first, then compress the fully-signed document. Compressing an unsigned document and then adding signatures changes the file again — and if you're signing digitally via an e-signature platform, the final exported PDF with embedded signatures is what you should compress.
Is ShrinkPDF safe for confidential tenancy agreements? ▼
Yes. ShrinkPDF processes all files locally in your browser — your rental agreement, IC details, salary figures, and property address are never sent to any server. This makes it safe for sensitive legal documents that you wouldn't want uploaded to a third-party platform.
