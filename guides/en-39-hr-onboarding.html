How to Compress a PDF for HR Onboarding

Starting a new job requires submitting a stack of documents — offer letter, employment contract, IC copy, academic certificates, EPF form, and more. HR systems typically limit uploads to 2MB–5MB per file. Here's how to compress each document correctly so everything submits smoothly on your first day.

Typical HR Onboarding Document Checklist

Most companies request some combination of the following on joining:

  • Signed offer letter or employment contract
  • IC / MyKad (front and back)
  • Academic transcripts and certificates (highest qualification)
  • Previous employment certificates or resignation letters
  • EPF (KWSP) member number / statement
  • Bank account details / void cheque
  • Passport-sized photo
  • Tax form (EA form or income tax number)
  • Emergency contact form
  • Medical examination report (some companies)

Which Compression Level for Each Document

  • Signed offer letter / employment contract: Balanced — signatures, salary, and terms must be legible
  • Academic transcript and certificates: Balanced — grades and institution details reviewed by HR
  • Previous employment certificate: Balanced — job title, dates, and company letterhead should be clear
  • IC / MyKad: Maximum — name and IC number are the key details
  • EPF statement: Balanced — account number and contribution figures reviewed
  • Bank details / void cheque: Balanced — account number and bank name must be exactly right
  • Medical examination report: Balanced — doctor's signature and findings should be clear
  • Emergency contact / personal data form: Maximum — text-heavy form, no critical images
Double-check bank account details before compressing

Your bank account number on the void cheque or bank details form must be 100% accurate — this is where your salary gets paid. Before compressing, verify the account number is visible and correct. After compressing with Balanced, check again that all digits are clearly readable.

Step-by-Step: Compress and Submit

1

Prepare All Documents

Gather every document on your company's onboarding checklist. For signed physical documents: scan them using your phone scanner app. For digital documents (offer letter from email, EPF statement from i-Akaun): download as PDF — these are usually already optimised and compress well.

2

Compress Each Document

Go to shrinkpdf.fyi and compress one document at a time using the levels above. Save all compressed files to a folder named "Onboarding Documents — Compressed".

3

Verify Critical Details

Open and verify: signatures are clear, bank account number is readable, IC number is correct, employment terms are legible. This prevents rejected submissions or payroll errors.

4

Submit to HR System or Email

Upload via your company's HR portal (Workday, SAP SuccessFactors, etc.) or email to HR. If emailing, attach all documents in one email with a clear subject line like "Onboarding Documents — [Your Name]".

Submit everything in one go

HR teams process many new joiners simultaneously. Sending all onboarding documents in a single email or portal submission — rather than separate emails per document — makes their job easier and speeds up your setup process.

Frequently Asked Questions

What file size do HR portals typically accept? ▼
Most HR portals (Workday, SAP SuccessFactors, Oracle HCM) accept files up to 2MB–10MB per document. Company-specific portals vary — some smaller companies use email with standard 25MB limits. To be safe, compress all onboarding documents to under 2MB each.
Is it safe to compress employment contracts on ShrinkPDF? ▼
Yes. ShrinkPDF processes all files locally in your browser — your salary details, employment terms, and personal information are never sent to any server. This is important for confidential employment documents.
Should I compress before or after signing the offer letter? ▼
After signing. Sign the offer letter (physically or digitally), scan or export it as a PDF with your signature included, then compress. Never compress a document and then add a signature — always compress the final signed version.
